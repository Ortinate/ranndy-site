Our Mandate

Overview

The Volta River Authority (VRA) was established in 1961 with the mandate to generate, transmit, and distribute electricity in Ghana and beyond. VRA is responsible for the management and operation of major hydroelectric, thermal, and renewable energy plants, ensuring reliable and sustainable power supply for economic growth and development.

Key Responsibilities

  • Generation of electricity from hydro, thermal, and renewable sources.
  • Transmission and distribution of electricity across Ghana and West Africa.
  • Operation and maintenance of the Akosombo and Kpong hydroelectric dams.
  • Development and management of thermal power stations and solar plants.
  • Promotion of sustainable and environmentally friendly energy solutions.
  • Support for national development through non-power activities and community initiatives.

Major Power Plants

  • Akosombo Hydro Power Plant
  • Kpong Hydro Power Plant
  • Takoradi Thermal Power Station
  • Tema Thermal Power Complex
  • Kpone Thermal Power Station
  • Navrongo Solar Power Plant
  • Lawra & Kaleo Solar Power Plants

Non-Power Activities

VRA also engages in non-power activities such as environmental management, resettlement, and community development projects to support the well-being of people living in areas affected by its operations.
