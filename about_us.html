About Volta River Authority (VRA)

Profile of VRA

The Volta River Authority (VRA) is a leading power generation company in Ghana and West Africa, established in 1961. VRA is responsible for the generation, transmission, and distribution of electricity, as well as the management of the Akosombo and Kpong hydroelectric dams. VRA also operates thermal and renewable energy plants, contributing significantly to the region’s energy supply.

Vision, Mission, Strategy & Values

Vision: To be a model of excellence for power utilities in Africa.
Mission: To power economies and raise living standards in Ghana and West Africa by providing reliable, safe, and environmentally friendly electricity and related services.
Values: Integrity, Innovation, Teamwork, Customer Focus, Safety, and Environmental Responsibility.

Board Chairman and Members

VRA’s Board comprises experienced professionals who provide strategic direction and oversight for the Authority’s operations and growth.

Ag. Chief Executive

The Acting Chief Executive leads VRA’s management team, ensuring the Authority’s objectives are met and its mandate fulfilled.

Management Team

VRA’s management team consists of skilled professionals dedicated to operational excellence, innovation, and customer satisfaction.

Power Generation

VRA operates hydro, thermal, and renewable energy plants, including the Akosombo and Kpong hydroelectric dams, Takoradi Thermal Power Station, and Navrongo Solar Power Plant.
