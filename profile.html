Profile of VRA

Mandate & History

The Volta River Authority (VRA) was established on April 26, 1961 with the mandate to generate, transmit and distribute electricity under the Volta River Development Act, Act 46 of the Republic of Ghana.

Following the 2005 amendment to the Act, VRA's mandate is now largely restricted to generation of electricity, enabling Independent Power Producers (IPPs) to enter the Ghana Energy market. Transmission was hived-off to Ghana Grid Company (GRIDCo), and distribution to Northern Electricity Distribution Company (NEDCo), a VRA subsidiary.

Power Activities

VRA has diversified its power generation portfolio to hydro, natural gas, and renewables, with a total installed capacity of 2,547MW. Akosombo (1,020MW) and Kpong (160MW) hydro plants are complemented by solar PV plants at Navrongo, Lawra, and Kaleo.

Thermal plants are located in Aboadze, Tema, and Anwomaso, including Takoradi Thermal (T1, 330MW), Takoradi International Company Thermal (T2, 340MW, JV with TAQA), Tema Thermal 1 (110MW), Tema Thermal 2 (80MW), Kpone Thermal (220MW), and Anwomaso Thermal (150MW, plus 100MW to be relocated).

Power Expansion Activities

VRA is re-powering the 132MW T3 Plant at Takoradi and converting Kpone Thermal into a combined cycle plant. Joint ventures and renewable energy policies are expanding solar and wind capacity, including Kaleo and Lawra solar projects and a 150MW wind project in the Volta Region.

The Pwalugu Multipurpose Dam Project includes a 60MW hydro plant, 50MW solar plant, and a 25,000-hectare irrigation scheme, improving power reliability and reducing flooding in the White Volta Basin.

Links with Customers & Neighbouring Countries

VRA reaches customers in Ghana and neighbouring countries via GRIDCo’s transmission system, linking with Cote d’Ivoire, Togo, and Burkina Faso under the West Africa Power Pool (WAPP). Major bulk customers include ECG, NEDCo, EPC, and various mining and industrial companies.

Sub-Regional Cooperation

VRA is a Foundation Customer in the West Africa Gas Pipeline (WAGP) and participates in regional power pooling under ECOWAS and WAPP. VRA is a founding member of the Association of Power Utilities of Africa (APUA).

Relations with Statutory Regulatory Bodies

VRA works with the Ministry of Energy, Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Public Enterprises, PURC, Energy Commission, and SIGA for regulatory and governance support.

Non-Power Activities

VRA administers Local Authority functions in Akosombo, Akuse, and Aboadze, manages schools and health care, and carries out environmental management including dredging, aquatic weed harvesting, and ecosystem restoration.

Climate Change Agenda

VRA’s Carbon Footprint Management Programme includes reforestation, waste segregation, climate-smart stoves, and ICT innovations for sustainable operations. All thermal plants now operate on natural gas, reducing carbon emissions.

Corporate Social Responsibility

VRA supports 52 resettlement townships through the VRA Resettlement Trust Fund and Community Development Programme (CDP), providing scholarships, socio-economic empowerment, sanitation, disease eradication, and public health initiatives.
